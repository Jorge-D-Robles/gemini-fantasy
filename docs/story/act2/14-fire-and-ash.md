# Chapter 14: Fire and Ash

**Act**: II — The Weight of Echoes
**Estimated Playtime**: 55-65 minutes
**Party**: Kael, Lyra, Iris, Garrick, Nyx, Cipher, Sienna; Ash joins end of chapter
**Locations**: Scorched Road, Emberhearth (Rim, Forge Quarter, The Deep), Ash Walker camp
**Emotional Arc**: Anticipation → culture shock → tenderness → dread

**Chapter Summary**: The party reaches the Cindral Wastes and the caldera city of Emberhearth, following Lyra's sense of the third Anchor. Garrick returns to a land that shaped him, and his knowledge of Ash Walker culture becomes essential. They find Ash — an eight-year-old who doesn't speak, protected by a nomadic Ash Walker clan who recognize the child as sacred. Ash communicates through emotional Resonance, felt rather than heard, and the party's first connection with them is one of the most tender scenes in the game. But the Shepherds have also tracked the Anchor here, and a siege force is gathering outside the caldera. The chapter ends with the ominous arrival of a Shepherd army, led by an Inquisitor who knows Garrick by name.

**JRPG Trope Subverted**: The "sacred child who speaks in prophecy." Ash isn't mystical or prophetic — they're a scared kid who can't talk and whose only language is making you feel what they feel. Their "powers" aren't awe-inspiring — they're overwhelming and isolating.

## Scene 1: Homecoming

**Location**: The Scorched Road, approaching Emberhearth
**Party**: Kael, Lyra, Iris, Garrick, Nyx, Cipher, Sienna
**Time**: Morning. Hot. The air shimmers with volcanic heat. Ash drifts like gray snow.
**Gameplay Context**: Overworld transition into Cindral Wastes region. New enemy types.
**Estimated Duration**: 5 minutes

### Setting

The Scorched Road is exactly what it sounds like — a cracked highway across a landscape of black rock, sparse vegetation, and thermal vents that hiss steam at irregular intervals. The sky is hazy with volcanic particulate. In the distance, the caldera of Emberhearth rises like a broken bowl against the sky, its rim dotted with structures and lights.

Garrick walks differently here. More carefully. He knows this ground.

### Return

*The heat hits them as they descend from the mountain pass. Cipher immediately pulls their coat tighter, which is wrong for the climate and right for the paranoia.*

**CIPHER**: The ambient temperature is thirty-eight degrees. The Resonance background radiation is elevated by roughly — *they touch the ground* — forty percent above baseline. How do people live here?

**GARRICK**: Carefully.

*He kneels at the roadside. Picks up a handful of volcanic soil. Lets it run through his fingers.*

**GARRICK**: Twenty years since I was last in the Wastes. *He looks at the caldera.* Emberhearth. I did my first Shepherd purge here. I was nineteen and convinced I was saving the world.

**KAEL**: What happened?

**GARRICK**: I burned a shrine. The Ash Walkers' central fire — their most sacred site. I put it out with purification crystal and told them they were free from the corruption of Resonance. *He stands.* They rebuilt it the next day. I came back and burned it again. They rebuilt it again. I came back a third time—

*He stops.*

**GARRICK**: And an old woman — the clan elder — she just looked at me. Didn't say a word. Didn't fight. Just looked at me like I was a child throwing a tantrum. And I realized that's exactly what I was.

**IRIS**: Did you burn it a third time?

**GARRICK**: ...Yes. I was nineteen and the Shepherd creed said crystal worship was sin. But I couldn't look at her again. I sent a subordinate.

*He adjusts his shield. The scratched-out Shepherd emblem catches the volcanic light.*

**GARRICK**: I'm going to walk into that city and I'm going to see people who remember what I did. They won't have forgotten. Ash Walkers don't forget.

**KAEL**: Will they help us?

**GARRICK**: They'll hear us out. The Ash Walkers value guests — even unwelcome ones. But help? That depends on whether I can face what I owe them.

### Gameplay Note

> Cindral Wastes exploration. Environmental hazard: Heat Exhaustion (party takes 1% HP damage per minute without heat protection). Heat Salve (purchasable in Emberhearth or craftable from Ember Fruit + Water) negates the effect. New enemies: Ash Stalkers, Magma Crawlers, Cinder Wisps. The caldera gates are visible — proceed to enter Emberhearth.

## Scene 2: Through the Gates

**Location**: Emberhearth — The Rim
**Party**: Full party
**Time**: Midday. The caldera provides shade but traps heat. Busy.
**Gameplay Context**: City arrival. Shops, NPCs, side quest hooks.
**Estimated Duration**: 8 minutes

### Setting

The Rim is the outer edge of Emberhearth — a ring of structures built along the caldera's lip. It's crowded: refugee tents, market stalls, repair shops, all under awnings of heat-resistant canvas. The people are diverse — native Ash Walkers in their geometric-patterned robes alongside refugees from every region. The air smells of sulfur, hot metal, and the smoke of a dozen different cooking fires.

Below the Rim, the caldera floor holds the old city — pre-Severance buildings half-buried in ash, their crystal spires still faintly glowing. And deeper still, the volcanic vent that gives Emberhearth its heat, its power, and its danger.

### Arrival

*The gates are guarded by Ash Walkers — tall, dark-skinned people with geometric ash markings on their faces and arms. They wear breathing masks pulled down around their necks. Their weapons are forged from volcanic glass.*

*One guard sees Garrick and freezes.*

**GATE GUARD**: ...Thorne.

**GARRICK**: Hello, Ember.

**GATE GUARD**: You've got nerve coming back to the caldera, old man. You know what they call you in the Rim? "Three-Burns Thorne." The Shepherd who couldn't take a hint.

**GARRICK**: I know.

**GATE GUARD**: *studying the group* You're not wearing the white anymore. Shield's defaced. That real, or costume?

**GARRICK**: It's real.

*The guard considers. Then steps aside.*

**GATE GUARD**: The elders will want to see you. All of you. Don't touch the sacred fires, don't disrespect the ash grounds, and don't — *she looks at Nyx's hooded form* — don't bring anything weird into the Deep without clearance.

**NYX**: *whispering to Kael* Am I weird?

**KAEL**: *whispering back* A little.

### Emberhearth Exploration

*The party enters the Rim. The energy is completely different from Gearhaven — where the Ironcoast city was mechanical and corporate, Emberhearth is organic and communal. People share food from common pots. Children play between tents. An old man teaches a group of teenagers to read volcanic glass formations.*

*Sienna takes notes on everything. Her scientist's brain can't help cataloguing.*

**SIENNA**: The thermal energy output from the caldera vent is — roughly — sufficient to power a city five times this size. The Ash Walkers are sitting on one of the largest natural power sources in the known world. And they use it for cooking and heating.

**IRIS**: Maybe they've got their priorities straight.

**SIENNA**: I wasn't criticizing. I was... admiring, actually. There's an elegance in restraint that the Initiative never learned.

*Cipher scans the area, visor down.*

**CIPHER**: Initiative has people here. I'm picking up two — no, three encrypted communication nodes in the city. Someone's watching.

**KAEL**: We knew they'd be here. Looking for the same thing we are.

**CIPHER**: Then we need to find the Anchor before they do.

### Gameplay Note

> Emberhearth is fully explorable. Shops: The Forge Quarter has the best blacksmith in the game (Master Forge — can craft unique volcanic weapons). The Rim has general supplies, an inn ("The Cooling Stone"), and an Echo trader. Side quests available: help refugees, investigate Initiative spies, excavation in The Deep. NPC dialogue reflects Garrick's history — some are hostile, some curious, some forgiving.

## Scene 3: The Ash Walkers' Fire

**Location**: Ash Walker clan camp, eastern caldera rim
**Party**: Kael, Garrick (others wait outside — cultural protocol)
**Time**: Afternoon. The central fire burns with blue-white volcanic heat.
**Gameplay Context**: Story scene. Garrick confronts his past. Information about Ash.
**Estimated Duration**: 10 minutes

### Setting

The Ash Walker camp is a circle of dome-shaped tents around a central fire pit. The fire burns constantly — volcanic gas channeled through carved stone pipes. It's been burning for centuries. The one Garrick extinguished three times.

Around the fire sit the clan elders — four old Ash Walkers with deeply lined faces and ash markings that cover their entire bodies. Between them sits an old woman who Garrick recognizes. She's older now — of course she is — but the eyes are the same.

Elder Kamara. The woman who looked at a nineteen-year-old zealot and saw a child.

### Facing the Fire

*Garrick enters the circle. Per Ash Walker custom, he kneels. Kael follows.*

*Kamara studies Garrick. She's missing three fingers on her left hand — crystal purification burns, old ones.*

**KAMARA**: Three-Burns. You got old.

**GARRICK**: So did you.

**KAMARA**: I earned my years. You owe yours.

*Garrick doesn't flinch.*

**GARRICK**: I do. I owe a debt to this fire and to this clan that I can never repay.

**KAMARA**: True. *She picks up a handful of ash from the fire pit. Holds it out to him.* You know the custom.

*Garrick takes the ash. Rubs it between his palms. Then draws it across his forehead — a mark of penitence in Ash Walker tradition.*

**KAMARA**: The fire forgives. The ash remembers. That is the Walker way. You are forgiven, Three-Burns. You are not forgotten.

*Something in Garrick's posture changes. Not relaxation — more like a weight redistributed.*

**GARRICK**: Thank you, Elder.

**KAMARA**: Don't thank me. I didn't do it for you. I did it because carrying grudges makes the walk harder, and this old woman's knees aren't what they were.

*She turns to Kael.*

**KAMARA**: And this one. *She studies them.* Your hands burn, don't they? I can see the crystal in your skin from here. You're not an Echo Hunter. You're something the fire hasn't named yet.

**KAEL**: We're looking for someone. A child. Someone who—

**KAMARA**: I know who you're looking for.

*She stands. The other elders make way.*

**KAMARA**: Come. Both of you.

### Gameplay Note

> Follow Kamara through the camp to a smaller tent at the edge. Garrick receives a special item: "Ash of Penitence" (accessory — +10% Defense, +10% Resistance, unique to Garrick). His dialogue updates reflect the weight of this scene.

## Scene 4: Ash

**Location**: Small tent at the edge of the Ash Walker camp
**Party**: Kael, Garrick, Elder Kamara
**Time**: Late afternoon. The tent is warm. Incense.
**Gameplay Context**: Meeting Ash. One of the most important character introductions in the game.
**Estimated Duration**: 8 minutes

### Setting

The tent is dim, lit by a single candle. Animal pelts on the floor. Drawings on the fabric walls — childish but expressive. Swirls of color. Faces with too many expressions. Landscapes that blend into each other.

Sitting cross-legged in the center, drawing with volcanic chalk on a flat stone, is a child.

Ash.

They're small — small for eight, even. Silver-white hair that drifts like it's underwater. Skin that shifts with faint crystalline patterns, pulsing with their heartbeat. They're barefoot, wearing simple Ash Walker clothing. Their eyes — currently focused on the drawing — shift color as they work. Blue. Gold. Green.

They don't look up when the tent flap opens.

### Meeting

*Kamara gestures for Kael and Garrick to sit. They do, quietly.*

*Ash continues drawing. The chalk moves with surprising confidence for small hands.*

**KAMARA**: *softly* Ash came to us two years ago. Found wandering the Scorched Road. No parents. No memory of where they came from. Couldn't speak — not wouldn't, couldn't. Something in them doesn't translate into words.

*She watches the child with unmistakable tenderness.*

**KAMARA**: But they speak in other ways. You'll feel it. Everyone does. Some people find it beautiful. Some find it terrifying. I find it both.

*Kael moves closer. Carefully, slowly, the way you approach a wild animal.*

*Ash looks up.*

*Their eyes settle on a warm amber — the same color as one of Kael's mismatched eyes. And then—*

*Kael feels it.*

*Not words. Not images. A wave of emotion that starts in their chest and spreads outward. Curiosity — bright, warm, tentative, like a hand reaching out in the dark. And underneath it, a question that Kael understands without hearing:*

*Who are you?*

*Kael's eyes sting. Not sadness — the intensity of being truly seen. Of someone asking who you are with no pretense, no agenda, just genuine wanting-to-know.*

**KAEL**: I... *Their voice catches.* I'm Kael. I'm — I'm like you, I think. I don't really understand what I am either.

*Ash tilts their head. The curiosity intensifies. And then a new feeling: recognition. Something in Ash recognizes something in Kael at a level beneath language, beneath thought.*

*Ash holds out the drawing they've been working on.*

*It's two figures — one large, one small — standing on opposite sides of a fire. But the fire connects them. It flows between them in arcs of color.*

*Kael takes the drawing. Their hands glow faintly — the crystalline scarring responding to Ash's proximity.*

**KAEL**: *to Kamara, barely above a whisper* They knew we were coming.

**KAMARA**: Ash always knows. They don't understand time the way we do. Past, present, future — it's all one feeling to them.

*Garrick has been standing by the tent entrance. His face is doing that thing it does — the complicated thing that leads to stillness. He's looking at Ash the way he might have looked at his own child.*

*Ash notices him. Sends something — not curiosity this time. Warmth. Comfort. The feeling of a blanket on a cold night.*

*Garrick's hand goes to his eyes. He turns away.*

**GARRICK**: ...Excuse me.

*He steps outside. Kamara watches him go.*

**KAMARA**: The fire forgives, but it doesn't lie. That child knows what's in your old Shepherd's heart. And what they found there was good. Tell him that, when he's ready to hear it.

### Gameplay Note

> Ash joins the party as a protected NPC (they do not participate in standard combat yet — they're eight years old). Ash's presence provides a passive buff: "Emotional Resonance" — party Resonance gauge builds 10% faster when Ash is in the party. Ash's profile is viewable but incomplete — their stats are listed as "???" and their abilities as "developing."

> Ash communicates through described emotional projections — felt by whoever is near them. The game represents this through narration boxes with no speaker label, colored differently from standard dialogue.

## Scene 5: The Warning

**Location**: The Rim marketplace, Emberhearth
**Party**: Full party (Ash stays with Kamara's clan)
**Time**: Evening. Market stalls closing. The caldera glows from below.
**Gameplay Context**: Gathering information about Initiative presence and incoming threat.
**Estimated Duration**: 6 minutes

### Setting

The evening market is winding down. Stalls fold up. Cooking fires light. The party regroups after exploring the city and meeting Ash. There's a sense of fragile peace — Emberhearth is alive and functional, the Ash Walkers have been welcoming, and Ash exists and is safe.

It won't last.

### The News

*Cipher finds the others at a food stall. They're moving faster than usual — which is saying something.*

**CIPHER**: We have a problem. Several problems. Ranked by severity.

**IRIS**: Go.

**CIPHER**: Problem one: the Initiative operatives in the city — I've been tracking their communications. They've identified Ash. They've sent for an extraction team. It'll be here in three days.

**KAEL**: We'll be gone before then. We take Ash and—

**CIPHER**: Problem two. *They pull down their visor, showing intercepted data.* Shepherd army. Coming from the south. Five hundred armed. Crystal purification weapons, siege equipment, and — here's the fun part — they're being led by an Inquisitor named Markus Hale.

*Garrick goes very still.*

**IRIS**: You know him?

**GARRICK**: I trained him. Twenty years ago. He was my protege in the Shepherd ranks. Fast learner. Dedicated. Absolute believer in the cause.

**CIPHER**: Well, your protege has a kill order. Direct from Prophet Null. "Eliminate Asset Ember at all costs. The third Anchor must not reach the heretics." That's us. We're the heretics.

**SIENNA**: Five hundred soldiers against a city of fifteen thousand, most of whom aren't fighters. The caldera walls provide natural defense, but if they have purification cannons—

**IRIS**: They won't need to breach the walls. Purification cannons can fire crystal-disruptive charges at range. Everything in Emberhearth that uses Resonance — which is everything — shuts down. Water purification, heating, medical equipment. They don't need to storm the city. They just need to kill it slowly.

*Silence.*

**KAEL**: How long do we have?

**CIPHER**: Shepherd army moves slow — heavy equipment. Two days, maybe three if the weather holds. If the weather breaks...

**GARRICK**: The Wastes don't break weather for anyone. Two days.

### Choice: What to Do About the Siege

**[A] Take Ash and leave before the army arrives**
> Protect the Anchor, abandon Emberhearth. Fastest option. Emberhearth suffers heavily. Garrick's bond decreases. Ash Walker relationship damaged.

**[B] Stay and help defend Emberhearth**
> Full siege battle sequence (Chapter 15). Riskier but protects the city. Garrick's bond increases. Ash Walker alliance strengthened. Unlocks unique side content.

**[C] Attempt to negotiate with the Shepherds before the siege**
> Garrick approaches under truce. Uses his history with Hale. Risky — may be captured. If successful, siege is reduced in scale. Requires Garrick+Kael bond at C-rank or higher.

## Scene 6: Two Days

**Location**: Various — Emberhearth preparation montage
**Party**: Full party, split across the city
**Time**: Two days compressed. Dawn to dusk to dawn.
**Gameplay Context**: Pre-battle preparation. Side quests, shopping, and story scenes.
**Estimated Duration**: 10 minutes (player-paced exploration)

### Setting

The city moves to a war footing. Ash Walkers sharpen volcanic glass weapons. Refugees stockpile water and food. Children are moved to the Deep — the underground shelters. Someone paints protective ash markings on the caldera walls. It's organized chaos, and at its center is a city that has survived volcanoes, the Severance, and three hundred years of hostile world, and has no intention of dying now.

### Preparation Vignettes

*Iris works with the city militia to reinforce the gate. She's in her element — engineering, fortification, practical solutions.*

**IRIS**: *to a group of defenders* The purification charges disrupt crystal-based systems. Anything powered by Resonance goes dead. So we go analog. Barricades — physical. Weapons — steel, volcanic glass, anything that doesn't need a power cell. If the lights go out, you fight in the dark. Understood?

*Cipher interfaces with the city's communication network, setting up an early warning system.*

**CIPHER**: *talking to themselves* ...Okay, crystal relay on the southern rim, signal bouncer on the eastern approach, and a data tap on the Initiative node — might as well listen to everyone while we're at it. Two-day deadline. Should have given me a real challenge.

*Sienna works with the city's healers, adapting her extraction knowledge for emergency medical protocols.*

**SIENNA**: The purification charges will cause acute Resonance disruption in anyone with even mild crystal integration. That's — *she counts* — approximately forty percent of the population. Symptoms: disorientation, nausea, temporary paralysis. The standard treatment is grounding crystals, but those won't work if the disruption field is active. We need chemical alternatives.

*She catches herself using clinical detachment and stops.*

**SIENNA**: What I mean is — a lot of people are going to feel very sick very quickly, and we need to be ready to help them.

*Garrick goes to the Ash Walker camp. He sits with the warriors. They don't talk to him at first. Then, slowly, they do.*

**ASH WALKER WARRIOR**: Three-Burns. I heard a rumor you're not terrible anymore.

**GARRICK**: I'm trying.

**ASH WALKER WARRIOR**: *hands him a cup of char wine* Then sit. Drink. And tell us about the Shepherd formations. How they march. Where they're weak. You owe us that much.

**GARRICK**: I owe you more than that. But it's a start.

*And Kael sits with Ash. Not talking — Ash can't talk. Not planning. Just sitting near the central fire while Ash draws. The child sends occasional pulses of emotion — calm, curiosity, a flicker of fear that passes quickly.*

*Kael draws too. They're terrible at it. Ash sends amusement.*

**KAEL**: Yeah, yeah. Not everyone's an artist.

*Ash sends warmth. The kind that says: It doesn't matter. You're here.*

### Gameplay Note

> Two-day preparation period. Player can explore Emberhearth freely, complete side quests, shop, and improve equipment. Time-limited quests: fortify the gates (reduces siege difficulty), set up medical stations (more healing during battle), and evacuate refugees to the Deep (protects civilian casualties). Each completed quest provides a mechanical benefit in Chapter 15's siege battle.

> Key purchases: Master Forge can create one unique volcanic weapon per party member. Heat Salve should be stocked. Ash-pattern armor (unique to Emberhearth) provides fire resistance.

## Scene 7: The Horizon

**Location**: Emberhearth southern rim, lookout point
**Party**: Kael, Garrick, Iris
**Time**: Dawn of the third day. Clear sky. Cold morning before the heat rises.
**Gameplay Context**: Chapter-ending scene. The Shepherd army arrives.
**Estimated Duration**: 5 minutes

### Setting

From the southern rim, you can see for miles across the Scorched Road and the surrounding wasteland. The sunrise paints everything orange and red. On a clear day, it's almost beautiful enough to forget where you are.

Today, the horizon moves.

### Arrival

*Kael, Garrick, and Iris stand at the lookout. Behind them, the city stirs — the sound of preparation, of a community girding itself.*

*On the horizon, dust. Too much dust for a caravan. Too organized for a storm.*

**IRIS**: *lowering a scavenged spyglass* I count... flags. White and gray. Shepherd colors. Twelve — no, fifteen unit pennants. That's roughly—

**GARRICK**: Five hundred. Like Cipher said.

**IRIS**: And siege equipment. I can see two purification cannons on wagon mounts. And—

*She adjusts the spyglass.*

**IRIS**: There's a figure out front. On a ridge. Separate from the army. Just... standing there.

*Garrick takes the spyglass. Looks. His face goes to granite.*

**GARRICK**: Markus.

*On the distant ridge, a lone figure in white and gray armor surveys Emberhearth. Even at this distance, there's a stillness about him — the kind of stillness Garrick used to have, before he learned that stillness and silence aren't the same as peace.*

**KAEL**: What do we do?

**GARRICK**: We fight. Or we run. Those are the two things you can do when someone comes for what you're protecting.

*He turns to Kael.*

**GARRICK**: Which one are we?

**KAEL**: *looking back at the city — the fires, the people, the child who draws pictures and speaks in feelings* We're the kind that stays.

*On the horizon, the army continues its slow approach. The dust cloud grows.*

### End Scene

*[Camera pulls back to show the full scope: tiny figures on the caldera rim, the city spread below them, and the approaching army — a slow, white line across the red earth. No music. Just wind and the distant sound of marching.]*

## Camp Scene: What Fire Means

**Location**: Kael and Ash at the central fire, night before the siege
**Party**: Kael and Ash (others preparing elsewhere)
**Time**: Late night. Stars visible through the volcanic haze. The fire pops.
**Estimated Duration**: 4 minutes

### Setting

The night before battle. Most of the city is trying to sleep. The Ash Walkers keep vigil around the central fire — it's tradition. Kael joins them, and Ash appears at their side without sound.

### Connection

*The fire burns blue-white. Hot enough to forge steel. The Ash Walkers who sit around it are silent — meditating, or praying, or just being present.*

*Ash sits next to Kael. Their eyes cycle slowly through colors — blue, gold, green, purple, settling on a deep amber.*

*They send a feeling: question. Not a specific question. The essence of questioning itself. What is happening? Why are people scared? Why does the horizon feel wrong?*

**KAEL**: *speaking softly, though Ash doesn't need words* There are people coming who want to hurt you. Not because you've done anything wrong. Because of what you are. Because they're scared of what you might become.

*Ash sends: confusion. Sadness. A small, aching bewilderment — the specific grief of being hated for existing.*

**KAEL**: I know. I know that feeling. I found out recently that I'm something people want to use or destroy, and I didn't choose it either.

*Ash reaches out and takes Kael's hand. Small fingers wrap around scarred ones. Kael's crystalline marks pulse gently in response to Ash's proximity.*

*And Ash sends something new. Not a question. Not an emotion.*

*An image. A memory. Two figures — one large, one small — standing in fire. But the fire doesn't burn them. It flows through them and out the other side, and where it exits, it becomes light.*

*Kael's breath catches.*

**KAEL**: Ash... did you just show me the future?

*Ash sends: uncertainty. The image wasn't the future. It was a hope. The difference matters to Ash, even if they can't explain why.*

*Kael squeezes their hand.*

**KAEL**: We're going to protect you. All of us. Whatever it takes.

*Ash sends: warmth. Trust. And underneath, buried so deep that Kael almost misses it — a fierce, quiet determination that seems far too large for such a small person.*

*The fire burns. The stars turn. Somewhere on the Scorched Road, an army sleeps.*

**Chapter Hook**: The Shepherd army arrives at Emberhearth. Five hundred soldiers, purification cannons, and an Inquisitor who was trained by Garrick himself. Inside the caldera, an eight-year-old child draws pictures by firelight and doesn't understand why the world wants them dead. The siege of Emberhearth is about to begin.

**Threads Advanced**:
- Ash found and introduced (nonverbal, emotional Resonance communication)
- Garrick's Shepherd past confronted directly (Kamara, ash of penitence)
- Inquisitor Markus Hale introduced (Garrick's former protege)
- Emberhearth established as a living, defended community
- Initiative spies in the city (secondary threat)
- Pre-battle preparations completed (mechanical benefits for Ch. 15)

- Ash's "image" of two figures standing in fire becoming light (Kael's Harmony ending)
- Garrick's relationship with Hale (will be the emotional center of Ch. 15)
- Sienna shifting from clinical to human language (her arc progressing)
- Cipher's discomfort in the heat (they're a city person — fish out of water)
- The central fire burning for 300 years (symbolic — the sacred persists through catastrophe)
- Ash's buried determination (they're not passive — they'll make choices later)
