# Chapter 3: The Deserter

**Location**: Verdant Tangle roads, eastern forest paths
**Party**: Kael -> Kael + Iris
**Estimated Playtime**: 50-65 minutes
**Emotional Arc**: Tension -> danger -> grudging trust -> camaraderie

On the road to the deeper ruins, Kael encounters a running fight. A woman in stripped-down military armor, one arm gleaming with Resonance-powered metal, cutting through forest like someone who knows exactly how many people are chasing her. Behind her, Initiative soldiers -- better armed, better organized, and very motivated. Kael makes a choice that can't be unmade, and Iris Mantle becomes the second person in the party. She doesn't trust easily, she swears constantly, and she's carrying secrets that make Lyra's look simple. But she can fight, she can think, and she has information about why the Initiative is so interested in the Verdant Tangle.

---

## Scene 1: The Road East

**Location**: Eastern forest path, deeper Verdant Tangle
**Party**: Kael
**Time**: Morning, foggy, visibility poor
**Gameplay Context**: Travel to deeper ruins, random encounters, foreshadowing
**Estimated Duration**: 5-8 minutes

### Setting

The forest east of Roothollow grows denser, darker. The trees are older here, trunks wider than houses, roots knotting together to form natural tunnels and bridges. Crystal growths are more frequent -- climbing up tree trunks in spiraling patterns, forming clusters that hum audibly. The fog is thick enough that visibility drops to twenty feet. Everything drips.

### Walking

*Kael moves through the fog with a lantern crystal in one hand and the journal in the other. They've marked the route from Morin's old map, but the terrain has shifted -- a bridge that was there twenty years ago has collapsed, a tunnel has been overgrown.*

**KAEL**: *(Consulting the map.)* "Eastern path, past the third stone waymarker." There's supposed to be a waymarker here. Unless the forest ate it. Which... yeah, the forest definitely ate it.

*They push through undergrowth. A crystal formation to their left pulses as they pass -- not threatening, just reactive.*

**KAEL**: The crystal density is way higher out here. Morin wasn't kidding about the deeper sectors being more active.

*They stop. Cock their head. Listen.*

*Through the fog, distant but unmistakable: metal on metal. Not the ring of a blacksmith. The clash of weapons.*

**KAEL**: ...That's not wildlife.

### Gameplay Note

> Player navigates the foggy forest path with reduced visibility. Encounters are with new enemies suited to the denser forest: Fog Stalkers (ambush-type, appear suddenly), Crystal Thorns (stationary but damaging if walked into). The sounds of fighting grow louder as the player progresses. Navigation uses simple pathfinding with Morin's map as a reference item.

## Scene 2: The Ambush

**Location**: Forest clearing, eastern path
**Party**: Kael
**Time**: Morning
**Gameplay Context**: Story encounter, introduction of Iris and Initiative soldiers
**Estimated Duration**: 8-10 minutes

### Setting

A clearing in the fog -- one of the rare open spaces where the canopy breaks enough for weak sunlight. It's been torn up. Scorch marks on the ground. Trees splintered by impact. And at the center, a fight that's been going for a while.

### The Fight

*Kael pushes through the last line of undergrowth and stops at the clearing's edge.*

*Three things register at once:*

*One: A woman, tall, dark-skinned, moving fast. She's wearing armor -- military-grade, but the insignias have been torn off, the paint scraped away. Her left arm catches the light wrong -- it's mechanical. Resonance-powered. Crystal conduits run through articulated metal plating. She's using it as both weapon and shield, blocking strikes and delivering counter-hits that crack tree trunks.*

*Two: Four soldiers in Initiative uniforms. White and gray, clean despite the forest. Professional. Two with Resonance rifles, one with a blade, one hanging back with what looks like a scanning device. They're coordinated. Trained. Closing in from three sides.*

*Three: A fifth soldier on the ground. Not moving. The woman did that.*

*The woman -- Iris -- parries a blade strike with her prosthetic arm, kicks the swordsman in the knee, turns, and puts herself behind a tree as two rifle shots blow chunks out of the trunk.*

**IRIS**: *(To no one in particular, breathing hard.)* Four. Down from five. Two ranged, one melee, one tech. Twenty meters to tree line. Low fog, poor visibility for them, worse for me. This is *not* how I wanted to spend my morning.

*The tech soldier speaks into a communicator.*

**INITIATIVE TECH**: Target is pinned in quadrant seven. Requesting reinforcements. She's taken out Corporal Dane's--

**IRIS**: *(From behind the tree.)* Dane's an idiot who telegraphed his swings. Tell your reinforcements I said hi.

*She rolls from cover, prosthetic arm reconfiguring -- the hand retracts, replaced by a compact energy emitter. She fires twice. One rifle soldier drops. The other ducks.*

*But the swordsman is flanking. Coming from her blind side.*

*Kael, at the clearing's edge, sees it happening. The swordsman raising his blade behind Iris. She hasn't noticed -- she's focused on the rifle.*

### Choice: Intervene

The player must decide:

**[A] Shout a warning**
-> Kael yells. Iris turns in time to block but not counterattack. The fight continues, 2-on-1 with Kael joining as backup after the swordsman recovers. Iris is grateful but irritated. "I had it handled. But thanks."

**[B] Attack the swordsman directly**
-> Kael charges from the tree line, catching the swordsman off-guard. A clean hit from behind staggers him. Kael enters the fight immediately. Iris is surprised but adapts instantly. "Who the hell are you? Never mind. Left flank."

**[C] Create a distraction with an Echo**
-> Kael releases an Echo fragment from the bag -- "Sparring Match" or any combat Echo. The burst of Resonance energy disorients the swordsman and the tech. Iris uses the opening to take out the swordsman. She stares at Kael. "You just threw a *memory* at them? That's the most overgrown thing I've ever seen."

*Regardless of choice, the result is the same: Kael and Iris fight together against the remaining Initiative soldiers.*

### Boss: Initiative Squad

> Combat encounter: 2-3 Initiative soldiers (depending on choice). This is the first combat with an ally -- Iris fights as a guest party member with preset actions. She's aggressive, favoring her prosthetic arm's built-in weapons and physical attacks. High damage, moderate defense.

> **Teaching purpose**: Introduces party combat -- coordinating two characters, covering each other's weaknesses. Iris targets the ranged enemies while Kael handles melee.

> **Enemies**: Initiative Rifleman (ranged, moderate HP), Initiative Swordsman (melee, high HP if still standing), Initiative Tech (support -- buffs allies, scans for weaknesses).

## Scene 3: Aftermath

**Location**: Forest clearing
**Party**: Kael, Iris (not yet party member)
**Time**: Morning, fog thinning
**Gameplay Context**: Character introduction, dialogue, trust negotiation
**Estimated Duration**: 8-10 minutes

### Setting

The clearing after the fight. Initiative soldiers unconscious or restrained with their own equipment. Iris is checking the downed soldiers' pockets with the efficiency of someone who's done this before. She hasn't looked at Kael directly yet.

### Meeting

*Iris finishes searching the last soldier. Pockets a communication crystal and a small data chip. Then she turns to Kael.*

*She's breathing hard. A cut on her right cheek. Her prosthetic arm has a fresh dent from a rifle butt. She's a head taller than Kael, and her eyes -- sharp, dark, missing nothing -- inventory them in about two seconds.*

**IRIS**: Three exits from this clearing. You came from the south. I came from the west. They came from the north, which means that's where their camp is, which means we're going east.

**KAEL**: I-- hi. I'm Kael.

**IRIS**: Great. Can you run?

**KAEL**: What?

**IRIS**: Run. With your legs. Fast. Because I called those "reinforcements" a minute ago and the Initiative doesn't bluff about reinforcements. We've got maybe ten minutes before a full tactical squad deploys from whatever forward base they've set up, and I'd rather not be here for that.

*She's already moving toward the eastern tree line.*

**KAEL**: Wait. Hold on. Who are you? What is the Initiative doing in the Verdant Tangle?

*Iris stops. Turns back. For a moment, her composure cracks -- just a sliver of exhaustion and something older underneath it.*

**IRIS**: My name is Iris Mantle. Former Lieutenant, Reclamation Initiative Engineering Corps. *Former* being the operative word since they decided I was an enemy of progress about two weeks ago. They're in the Verdant Tangle because they're looking for something specific in the deep ruins, and they're hunting me because I know what that something is and they'd really prefer I stopped breathing.

*Her prosthetic hand clenches -- the fingers articulate with a faint mechanical whine, a tell she probably doesn't know she has.*

**IRIS**: I can explain more while we move. Preferably far from here. Are you coming or not?

*Kael looks at the unconscious soldiers. Looks at Iris.*

**KAEL**: East works. I was heading that way anyway.

## Scene 4: Running Conversation

**Location**: Eastern forest, dense undergrowth
**Party**: Kael, Iris
**Time**: Late morning
**Gameplay Context**: Dialogue while moving, world-building, character development
**Estimated Duration**: 5-7 minutes

### Setting

Dense forest. Kael and Iris move fast through the undergrowth, ducking branches, leaping roots. Iris moves like someone with military training -- checking angles, scanning the canopy, never staying still for longer than necessary.

### On the Move

*They jog through the forest. The conversation happens in bursts, between heavy breathing and navigation.*

**IRIS**: The Initiative has been pushing into the Tangle for months. Small teams. Survey units, officially. "Mapping crystal deposits for future development." Bull.

**KAEL**: The merchant Reva told me about survey teams. Three in the last month.

**IRIS**: More like six. Three you heard about. Three you didn't, because they went dark. The forest took them, or the Echoes did, or they found what they were looking for and went quiet on purpose.

**KAEL**: What are they looking for?

**IRIS**: Resonance anomalies. Specifically, high-coherence crystal formations with consciousness indicators.

*Kael's stomach drops.*

**KAEL**: Consciousness indicators.

**IRIS**: Yeah. The same impossible thing that the Initiative has been chasing for five years. Crystal formations that think. Conscious Echoes. The Director -- Vex Thornwright -- he's obsessed. Thinks a Conscious Echo could be the key to rebuilding the Resonance network. A living template for collective consciousness.

*She vaults a fallen log without breaking stride.*

**IRIS**: I was on the research team. Engineering side. Building the equipment to detect and extract Conscious Echoes. Resonance sensors, containment arrays, the whole toolkit. State of the art. Extremely classified.

**KAEL**: And you deserted because...

*Iris slows. Stops behind a massive tree trunk. Peers around it, checking the path ahead. When she speaks, her voice is flatter.*

**IRIS**: Because I found out what they do with the ones they find. The fragments, the formations, the traces of consciousness. They don't study them. They don't preserve them. They *use* them. Feed them into the Resonance Cage prototype like fuel. Every Conscious Echo the Initiative has found in five years is gone. Consumed. For Director Vex's pet project.

*She turns to Kael.*

**IRIS**: I built the extraction tools. I helped them find those Echoes. And then I found out what happened next, and I-- couldn't. So I took the data on a chip, walked out of the lab, and started running.

*She holds up the data chip she pulled from the downed soldier.*

**IRIS**: They want this back. And me quiet. Preferably permanently.

*Kael is very still. The color has left their face.*

**KAEL**: Iris.

**IRIS**: What?

**KAEL**: I need to tell you something. And I need you to not freak out.

**IRIS**: I am a professional. I don't freak out. I assess and respond.

**KAEL**: I found a Conscious Echo. Two days ago. In the south ruins. She's alive. Her name is Lyra.

*Iris stares.*

**IRIS**: ...You found one.

**KAEL**: She spoke to me. In present tense. She's a pre-Severance scientist who--

**IRIS**: Where. Exactly where.

**KAEL**: Sector Seven. Preserved room with a crystal dome. She's fragmented but coherent. I found one of her other fragments yesterday in the old market district.

*Iris processes this. Her eyes do something Kael hasn't seen before -- the calculation in them gives way to something rawer.*

**IRIS**: Then the Initiative is going to find her. They're surveying this sector. Their sensors will pick up consciousness indicators and they'll come for her, and they'll--

*She stops herself.*

**IRIS**: How far is she from here?

**KAEL**: A few hours west. Back toward Roothollow.

**IRIS**: Then we need to move. Now. Before the survey teams reach that sector.

**KAEL**: We?

*Iris looks at Kael. Really looks at them.*

**IRIS**: You saved my life back there. Or at least you saved me a broken rib. And you've got a Conscious Echo that the Initiative will burn to fuel their nightmare machine. I'm already running from these people. Might as well run toward something.

*Beat.*

**IRIS**: Also, no offense, but you fight like someone who learned from a book. You need someone watching your back who knows which end of a rifle is which.

**KAEL**: I fight fine.

**IRIS**: You left your left side open three times during the clearing fight. I counted.

**KAEL**: *(Pause.)* ...Okay, maybe I could use some help.

### Gameplay Note

> Iris Mantle joins the party as a full party member. Her character stats, equipment, and abilities are now available. She starts with: Modified Initiative Armor (stripped insignias), her cybernetic left arm (counts as weapon -- can switch between melee strike and energy emitter), and basic supplies she scavenged from the soldiers. She has the abilities: ARM STRIKE (physical melee), PULSE SHOT (ranged energy), and FIELD REPAIR (heal self, small amount).

## Scene 5: Making Camp

**Location**: Hidden grotto, eastern forest
**Party**: Kael, Iris
**Time**: Afternoon
**Gameplay Context**: First camp scene with a party member, bonding, exposition
**Estimated Duration**: 8-10 minutes

### Setting

A natural grotto formed by tree roots arching over a crystal-fed stream. The water glows faintly blue. Iris declared it "defensible -- two exits, overhead cover, water source, natural sound dampening" and then sat down like someone who hasn't stopped moving in days.

### The Camp

*Kael builds a small fire with practiced hands. Iris sits against a root, watching the two entry points alternately. Her prosthetic arm is extended, a panel open, and she's making adjustments with a tiny tool from her belt.*

**KAEL**: So. Former Initiative engineer.

**IRIS**: Yeah.

**KAEL**: From Gearhaven.

**IRIS**: Born and raised.

**KAEL**: What's that like?

**IRIS**: Big. Loud. Everything smells like machine oil and ambition. The harbor is actually beautiful at sunset, if you can see it through the smog. *(Beat.)* I haven't been back in two weeks. Don't plan to.

*She tightens something in the arm. It whirs, recalibrates.*

**KAEL**: How did you end up in the Engineering Corps?

**IRIS**: Tested high for Resonance aptitude at fourteen. Initiative recruiter showed up at my school the next day. Offered a full scholarship to the Technical Academy. Better than anything the public schools could do, and my family didn't have the RC for private. So.

*She closes the panel.*

**IRIS**: Twelve years. Academy, then the Corps. Rose to Lieutenant. Designed three generations of Resonance extraction equipment. My work was "exemplary." That's what the commendations said. "Exemplary contribution to the Reclamation Initiative's core mission."

*Her voice goes flat.*

**IRIS**: "Core mission" meaning finding Conscious Echoes and feeding them into a machine that strips them down to raw Resonance energy. I just didn't know that part until I walked into the wrong lab on the wrong day.

**KAEL**: What did you see?

*Iris looks at the fire. The reflected flames move in her eyes and in the crystal conduits of her prosthetic arm.*

**IRIS**: A containment unit. Inside it, a Conscious Echo. Not as coherent as your Lyra -- more fragmented, more damaged. But aware. It could see me through the glass. It was pressing its hands against the inside of the unit and its mouth was moving and no sound was coming out.

*She swallows.*

**IRIS**: The technicians said it was just "residual consciousness patterns mimicking emotional response." Not real feeling. Not real awareness. Just an echo of an echo.

*She meets Kael's eyes.*

**IRIS**: It was crying. Whatever the hell "residual consciousness patterns" means, it was crying. And the next morning it was gone. Processed. Fed into the Cage prototype like firewood.

[SILENCE -- The stream murmurs. The fire crackles.]

**KAEL**: I'm sorry.

**IRIS**: Don't be sorry. Be angry. Sorry doesn't help. Anger gets things done.

*She picks up a stone from the stream bed. Turns it in her prosthetic hand. The metal fingers are precise enough to feel its texture.*

**IRIS**: I took everything I could grab -- research data, sensor logs, extraction protocols -- and I walked out. Didn't even have a plan. Just... walked. Figured I'd get to the Tangle, disappear into the forest, find the Free Resonants and hand them the data. Instead I got four squads of Initiative hunters and two weeks of not sleeping.

**KAEL**: The Free Resonants?

**IRIS**: Decentralized resistance movement. Scholars, refugees, idealists. They believe humans and crystals can coexist without anyone getting exploited or erased. I don't know if I believe that, but they're the only people who'd care about what's on this chip.

*She holds up the data chip.*

**IRIS**: Everything the Initiative knows about Conscious Echoes. Detection methods, extraction protocols, Cage specifications. In the right hands, it's a blueprint for protecting every Conscious Echo in the world. In the wrong hands...

**KAEL**: It's a blueprint for hunting them.

**IRIS**: Exactly.

*Kael stares at the fire.*

**KAEL**: Lyra. If the Initiative finds her...

**IRIS**: They won't. Because we're going to get her fragments first, and then we're going to figure out how to move her somewhere they'll never look.

*She pauses.*

**IRIS**: "We." Listen to me. I've known you for three hours.

**KAEL**: It's been a big three hours.

*Iris almost smiles. It's a small, reluctant thing, surprised out of her.*

**IRIS**: Your Lyra. This secret she's protecting about the Severance. Does she have any idea how many fragments are left?

**KAEL**: She doesn't know exactly. She was pretty vague. "Scattered across the ruins, maybe farther."

**IRIS**: Then here's what we do. Tomorrow, we go back to the preserved room. I scan the area with Initiative sensors -- I know their frequencies, I can modify them to detect Lyra's specific resonance signature. If her other fragments have the same signature, I can build a tracker.

**KAEL**: You can do that?

**IRIS**: I spent twelve years building these tools. Might as well use them for something that doesn't make me sick.

### NPC Dialogue: Iris Camp Conversation (Optional)

The Initiative gave me the prosthetic -- top of the line, Resonance-integrated, full sensory feedback. I think they did it so I'd feel grateful. Loyal. "Look what we gave you."

*She flexes the metal fingers.*

"Joke's on them. It's the best thing they ever built, and now I'm using it to fight them."

**IRIS** *(about Kael)*: You're not what I expected from an Echo hunter. Most of them are mercenaries. Grab the fragment, sell it to whoever pays, move on. You're... something else.

**KAEL**: I just think memories matter.

**IRIS**: Yeah. *(Quiet.)* They do.

**IRIS** *(about Roothollow)*: You grew up here? In the trees?

**KAEL**: Sort of. Morin -- the village elder -- found me when I was a kid. Near the Hollows. No memories, no family, no nothing. He brought me to Roothollow and I just... stayed.

**IRIS**: No memories at all?

**KAEL**: Not from before Morin. Just dreams that don't make sense.

*Iris looks at Kael differently. Something behind the calculation.*

**IRIS**: Huh. *(She doesn't press.)* Get some rest. I'll take first watch.

## Scene 6: Night Watch

**Location**: Hidden grotto
**Party**: Kael (sleeping), Iris (on watch)
**Time**: Night
**Gameplay Context**: Non-interactive character moment, Iris solo
**Estimated Duration**: 2-3 minutes

### Setting

The grotto at night. Fire burned down to embers. Crystal stream still glowing. Kael asleep in the hammock they strung between roots. Iris sits at the grotto entrance, prosthetic arm resting on her knee, eyes on the dark forest.

### Alone

*Iris pulls out the data chip. Turns it over in her metal fingers. The crystal in it catches the ember light.*

*She also pulls out something else from an inner pocket: a photograph. Worn, creased, real paper -- rare and valuable in Aethermoor. Two people in Initiative uniforms, young, grinning. Iris and a young man who looks like a younger, softer version of her.*

*She looks at the photo for a long time.*

**IRIS**: *(Very quietly.)* I hope you're okay, Dane.

*She puts the photo back. Picks up the data chip. Weighs it in her hand.*

**IRIS**: *(To herself.)* You chose to run. This is what running looks like. Sleeping under a tree with a stranger who talks to crystals.

*She looks back at Kael, sleeping peacefully.*

**IRIS**: Could be worse.

*She turns back to the darkness and settles in for the watch.*

## Scene 7: Morning -- The Tracker

**Location**: Hidden grotto, then return path toward preserved room
**Party**: Kael, Iris
**Time**: Early morning
**Gameplay Context**: Gameplay mechanics introduction (Iris's tracking ability), travel
**Estimated Duration**: 5-8 minutes

### Setting

Morning light slanting through the root canopy. The stream sings. Iris is already awake, sitting cross-legged with components spread around her: pieces from her belt kit, salvaged Initiative tech, and the crystal from the downed soldier's scanner.

### Building the Tracker

*Kael wakes to the sound of tiny tools clicking.*

**KAEL**: *(Groggy.)* What time is it?

**IRIS**: Dawn. Plus twenty. I didn't want to wake you but I'm almost done and I need your Echo.

**KAEL**: My what?

**IRIS**: Lyra's fragment. The piece you still have. I need to calibrate the tracker to her specific Resonance signature.

*Kael sits up. Watches Iris work. Her prosthetic hand is dismantled at the wrist -- she's using its internal sensors as the core of a handheld device cobbled together from the Initiative scanner and her own components.*

**KAEL**: You took apart your own hand.

**IRIS**: It reattaches. Hold still. I need to scan you too.

**KAEL**: Me?

**IRIS**: You're resonating with Lyra's fragment. I noticed it last night. Your natural frequency has shifted toward hers -- subtle, but detectable. If I can map the interference pattern, I can filter it out of the tracker's input and get a cleaner signal for her other fragments.

*She holds a crystal sensor near Kael's chest. It hums.*

**IRIS**: ...Huh.

**KAEL**: What?

**IRIS**: Your base Resonance frequency is extremely high. Like, off-the-chart high. The Initiative would classify you as a Category Five emitter.

**KAEL**: Is that bad?

**IRIS**: It's rare. I've seen maybe three Category Fives in twelve years. *(She looks at Kael with new wariness, then shakes it off.)* It explains why Lyra responded to you. You're basically a walking Resonance battery. She could feel you coming from a mile away.

*She finishes calibrating. Reattaches her hand. Holds up the tracker: a small crystal device that pulses faintly in rhythm with Lyra's fragment.*

**IRIS**: There. Fragment tracker, keyed to Lyra's signature. When we're within range of one of her pieces, this will pulse faster. Direction-sensitive -- stronger pulse means closer.

**KAEL**: That's incredible. You built that in an hour.

**IRIS**: It's basic field engineering. I used to build these in my sleep. *(Beat.)* The Initiative taught me well. They just didn't teach me when to stop.

### Gameplay Note

> New party mechanic: Fragment Tracker. A UI element (small crystal icon) appears on-screen that pulses when the party is near one of Lyra's fragments. Directional -- pulses faster as you approach the correct direction. This becomes the primary navigation tool for the fragment collection quest. Iris has also unlocked a new ability: SCAN (reveals enemy weaknesses and hidden items in the environment).

## Scene 8: Return to Lyra

**Location**: Preserved room, South Ruins
**Party**: Kael, Iris
**Time**: Late morning
**Gameplay Context**: Iris meets Lyra, party dynamics established
**Estimated Duration**: 6-8 minutes

### Setting

The preserved room. Crystal formations humming gently. Kael enters first; Iris follows, scanning the room with professional eyes and evident discomfort.

### Introduction

*Kael places their hand on the central formation.*

**KAEL**: Lyra? I'm back. And I brought someone.

*The formation brightens. Lyra's form materializes -- more solid than last time, thanks to the integrated fragment, but still translucent. She looks at Iris.*

*Iris takes one step back. Her prosthetic arm twitches, an unconscious defensive reflex.*

**LYRA**: You've brought company. *(Her gaze moves to Iris's arm.)* Initiative engineering. Third-generation Resonance prosthetic with integrated sensor array and modular tool mounting. That's excellent work.

**IRIS**: *(Stiff.)* Former Initiative. And thanks. I designed the sensor integration myself.

**LYRA**: *(With genuine interest.)* You did? The harmonic coupling between the crystal conduits and the neural interface -- that was the hardest problem in pre-Severance prosthetics. We never solved it cleanly. You did?

**IRIS**: *(Thrown off.)* I... yeah. Took me two years. Resonance frequency matching with adaptive feedback loops. It's not perfect, but--

**LYRA**: It's remarkable. Can I-- *(She catches herself. Her translucent hand is reaching toward Iris's arm.)* I'm sorry. I forget I'm not in a lab anymore. Or a body.

*Iris stares at her.*

**IRIS**: You're real.

**LYRA**: So I'm told.

**IRIS**: I mean -- the Initiative's been searching for Conscious Echoes for years and they never found anything this... complete. This coherent. You're having a conversation. You recognized my prosthetic's generation. You made a joke.

**LYRA**: I've had three hundred years to work on my material.

*Iris looks at Kael.*

**IRIS**: She's real.

**KAEL**: That's what I said.

*Iris turns back to Lyra. Something shifts in her expression -- the professional wariness giving way to something more complicated. She pulls out the data chip.*

**IRIS**: Lyra. My name is Iris Mantle. I was a Lieutenant in the Initiative Engineering Corps until two weeks ago. This chip contains everything the Initiative knows about Conscious Echoes, including the fact that every one they've ever found has been destroyed. Fed into a prototype they call the Resonance Cage.

*Lyra's form flickers. Not from energy loss -- from shock.*

**LYRA**: Destroyed?

**IRIS**: Consumed. Used as Resonance fuel for a device that's supposed to rebuild the global consciousness network. Director Vex Thornwright's project. His life's work.

*Lyra is quiet for a long moment. The crystal formations around her dim.*

**LYRA**: I sealed myself away because I was afraid someone would use what I know. I didn't realize they were already using what I *am*.

**IRIS**: That's why I'm here. I'm not going to let that happen to you. *(She looks at Kael.)* We're not going to let that happen.

*Lyra looks between them. Her form stabilizes.*

**LYRA**: Then we need to find my fragments quickly. Before the Initiative reaches this sector. I can feel three -- no, four -- more within the Tangle. Others might be farther. But the closest is in the Overgrown Capital, in the old research quarter. That's where I worked. That's where the most important memories will be.

**IRIS**: I've got a tracker keyed to your signature. We can find them.

**LYRA**: *(Looking at Iris with warmth.)* You built a tracker? From field components? In a morning?

**IRIS**: It's basic engineering.

**LYRA**: It's extraordinary engineering. *(Beat.)* You remind me of someone I used to work with. She could build anything from scraps and then call it "basic."

*Iris doesn't know what to do with the compliment. She looks away.*

**IRIS**: We should move. The Initiative won't stay confused forever.

## Camp Scene: Kael and Iris

**Location**: Camp near the ruins, evening
**Party**: Kael, Iris
**Time**: Evening
**Estimated Duration**: 4-5 minutes

### Two Strangers, One Fire

*Camp. Iris has set up a perimeter -- small crystal sensors at both approaches, rigged to chime if anything crosses them. She sits cleaning her prosthetic with a cloth.*

*Kael is sketching in the journal.*

**KAEL**: Can I ask you something?

**IRIS**: Depends.

**KAEL**: Your arm. Does it hurt?

*Iris pauses.*

**IRIS**: The arm itself? No. Full sensory feedback means I feel pressure, temperature, texture. No pain signals. That was a design choice. *(Beat.)* The place where it connects -- the stump -- that aches sometimes. Especially when the weather changes. And sometimes I feel the old hand. The one that's not there. Phantom nerve signals.

**KAEL**: That sounds awful.

**IRIS**: It's not great. But the alternative was no arm at all, so. *(She holds up the prosthetic, flexing the fingers.)* I made my peace with it. The arm is better than what I lost. Stronger, more precise, doesn't get tired.

*She pauses.*

**IRIS**: Doesn't feel the same, though. When I touch something with my right hand, it's touch. Warmth, texture, the whole thing. When I touch something with the left... it's data. Pressure readings and temperature values and surface analysis. Accurate, but not the same.

*She looks at the cloth in her metal hand.*

**IRIS**: I can tell you this cloth is cotton, twelve-thread count, moisture content seven percent. But I can't tell you what it *feels* like. Not the way my other hand can.

**KAEL**: That's the difference between data and experience.

**IRIS**: *(Looking at Kael, surprised.)* Yeah. That's exactly what it is.

*Beat.*

**IRIS**: The Initiative doesn't understand that difference. They think if you can quantify something, you've captured it. That's why they think a Conscious Echo can be "extracted" without loss. Reduce consciousness to data, feed it into the Cage, rebuild the network from numbers.

**KAEL**: But consciousness isn't numbers.

**IRIS**: Try telling them that. I did. Look where it got me.

*She wraps the cloth around her prosthetic wrist -- a small, habitual gesture, like someone adjusting a bracelet.*

**KAEL**: For what it's worth, I'm glad you're here.
